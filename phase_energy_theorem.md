The phase twist θ_y modulates transitions: θ_y = arcsin(⋒ ΔE / (R p_y)), where:
- ΔE = energy difference between levels (eV or J)
- ⋒ = universal scaling constant (derived from calibration)
- R = 5D compact radius
- p_y = 5D momentum scale

Wavelength:
$$
\lambda = \frac{h c}{\Delta E} = \frac{h c}{(\theta_y / ⋒) (R p_y)}

$$
\lambda = \frac{K}{\sin(\theta_y)} \quad \text{(in ⋓)}

$$
\theta_y \approx ⋒ \Delta E
$$
$$
\lambda \approx \frac{K}{⋒ \Delta E} \quad \text{(in ⋓)}
$$

⋒ calibrated from a reference transition (e.g., Hydrogen H-alpha).
